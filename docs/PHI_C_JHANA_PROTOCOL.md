# Φc / jhāna phenomenology protocol

A short, citable protocol for testing MQGT-SCF predictions that contemplative states (e.g. jhānas) correlate with measurable signatures interpretable as Φc–E attractor dynamics. Suitable as a methods supplement or standalone pre-registration.

---

## 1. Objective

To test whether neurophysiological and behavioral measures in contemplative practitioners (e.g. during jhāna or sustained attention states) show patterns consistent with MQGT-SCF: specifically, that stable contemplative states correspond to attractor-like dynamics in a reduced phase space that can be proxied by coherence and stability measures (Φc-related) and by outcome-valence or ethical-weight proxies (E-related). The protocol does not assume the framework is true; it defines outcome variables and analysis so that positive or null results are interpretable.

---

## 2. Design

- **Population:** Experienced meditators (e.g. jhāna practitioners or equivalent) and matched controls; sample size to be set by power analysis for primary outcomes.
- **Neurophysiology:** MEG and/or SQUID-based magnetometry to capture high-temporal-resolution neural activity. Regions and frequency bands of interest to be specified (e.g. alpha/gamma coherence, default-mode vs. task-positive balance) as operational proxies for “coherence” and “stability” in the framework.
- **Behavioral / self-report:** Standardized scales for depth of absorption, clarity, and (if applicable) ethical/valence content; timestamps aligned with MEG/SQUID blocks. Pre/post session ratings to capture sustained shifts.
- **Conditions:** Resting baseline, neutral task, and contemplative (jhāna or equivalent) blocks; within-subject where feasible. Order and duration fixed in protocol to limit experimenter degrees of freedom.
- **Blinding:** Analysis team can be blind to condition labels until primary analysis is locked; pre-registration reduces p-hacking and expectancy effects.

---

## 3. Outcome variables and mapping to Φc–E attractor predictions

- **Primary (Φc-related):** Coherence and stability metrics derived from MEG/SQUID (e.g. phase-locking, power in specified bands, variance over time). Prediction: contemplative blocks show higher coherence and/or lower variance in these metrics than baseline/control, consistent with movement toward a stable attractor in a reduced dynamical space.
- **Secondary (E-related):** If self-report or behavioral tasks include valence/ethical content, prediction: contemplative blocks show a shift toward higher E-proxy scores (e.g. coherence with prosocial or “unified” valence) relative to baseline.
- **Null hypothesis:** No systematic difference between contemplative and control conditions in the primary or secondary outcomes. A pre-registered analysis plan will define the exact test (e.g. mixed-effects model, cluster permutation) and decision rule.

Mapping to MQGT-SCF: jhānas (or equivalent) are modeled as attractors in the Φc–E phase space; MEG/SQUID and behavioral measures are operational proxies for proximity to those attractors, not direct measurements of Φc or E fields.

- **Where:** OSF (osf.io) or equivalent (e.g. AsPredicted, ClinicalTrials.gov for clinical components if applicable).
- **When:** Before data collection or before unblinding analysis. The pre-registration should include: hypothesis, primary/secondary outcomes, analysis plan, sample size justification, and exclusion criteria.
