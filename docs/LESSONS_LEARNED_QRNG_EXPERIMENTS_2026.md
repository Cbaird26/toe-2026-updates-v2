# Lessons Learned from the QRNG Experiments (2026)

**Cross-phase summary for future empirical work.** Christopher Michael Baird | Baird–ZoraASI Collaboration | March 2026

---

## Scope

Phase I (baseline, meditation intervention) and Phase II (100k swarm/coherence pilots) are closed. This note captures what the QRNG program validated, what it did not show, and what to carry forward.

---

## What the program validated

1. **Pipeline and method** — ANU API integration, key handling, preflight, artifact naming, and recovery from logs are stable. The method doc ([QRNG_METHOD_MARCH_2026_DO_NOT_CHANGE.md](QRNG_METHOD_MARCH_2026_DO_NOT_CHANGE.md)) is the single source for replication.

2. **Experimental discipline** — Pre-specified decision rules, replication before scaling, and a clear stop rule were defined and followed. When Pilot 3 failed to replicate Pilot 2, the ladder stopped; no escalation to 1M+.

3. **Null results as outcomes** — Phase I and Phase II both produced null or non-replicated results. Those outcomes are documented, cross-linked, and committed. The work is falsification-relevant: specific protocols were tested and did not show an effect.

4. **Documentation chain** — Trace map, results summary, runbook, close-out, and research index give an audit trail that reviewers and future you can follow.

---

## What we did not find

- No reproducible effect of meditation intervention on QRNG statistics (Phase I).
- No reproducible swarm/coherence effect at 100k (Phase II). Pilot 2’s directional hint reversed in Pilot 3; treated as noise.

So: the QRNG component has served its purpose as an exploratory branch. The hypothesis (consciousness-related influence on QRNG output) was not supported under these protocols.

---

## What to carry forward

- **Method lock** — For any future QRNG work, keep the March 2026 method doc unchanged; replication and comparison depend on it.
- **Stop rules first** — Define “replicate or stop” and “do not scale without replication” before running pilots. We did; it prevented scaling a non-replicated effect.
- **Protocol audit before redesign** — If Phase II-style work continues, do not scale the same protocol. Use a protocol audit ([QRNG_SWARM_PROTOCOL_AUDIT_2026.md](QRNG_SWARM_PROTOCOL_AUDIT_2026.md)) and a new pilot design.
- **One place for “what we’re advancing”** — The research index ([RESEARCH_ADVANCE_INDEX_2026.md](RESEARCH_ADVANCE_INDEX_2026.md)) keeps QRNG, consciousness protocol, Block 8, and governance in one map. Keep it updated.

---

## Bottom line

The QRNG experiments are a clean example of: hypothesis → pilot → hint → replication attempt → null/non-replication → stop rule → archive. That sequence is good practice. The value is in the closed branch and the lessons, not in a positive result. When the project evolves, this note is the short reference for what was tried, what was learned, and what to do differently next time.
